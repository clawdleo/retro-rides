# Pat Ina Brand Guidelines

## Brand Identity

### Name
**Pat Ina** (stylized: *Pat Ina* or **PAT INA**)
- Evokes "patina" - the authentic aged character of vintage bikes
- Simple, memorable, works in any language

### Tagline Options
- "Preserved, Not Polished"
- "Honest Iron"
- "Where Steel Has Stories"

### Voice & Tone
- **Knowledgeable** but not snobby
- **Passionate** but not preachy
- **Direct** - no fluff, respect people's time
- **Authentic** - show the flaws, be honest about condition
- Use "I" not "we" - this is personal, not corporate

### Visual Style
- **Colors:** Warm earth tones - rust, copper, aged bronze, cream
- **Photography:** Natural light, simple backgrounds, detail shots
- **Aesthetic:** Clean, minimal, lets the bikes speak
- **No:** Heavy filters, cluttered backgrounds, staged perfection

## Photography Guidelines

### Equipment
- Phone camera is fine (modern phones)
- Natural light (golden hour is chef's kiss)
- Clean background (concrete, wood, nature)

### Required Shots per Bike
1. Full bike, drive side (the "hero shot")
2. Full bike, non-drive side
3. Head tube / brand badge
4. Rear triangle detail
5. Component group (shifters, derailleurs)
6. Any flaws / damage (honesty builds trust)
7. Serial number (for provenance)

### Style Tips
- Slightly lower angle makes bikes look better
- Negative space is your friend
- Show scale (in use, or against something)
- Detail shots: fill the frame
