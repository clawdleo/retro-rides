Step 1/9

Preserved, Not Polished

Every bike here carries its history. The scratches from rocky descents. The worn decals from decades of storage. The honest patina of a machine that was ridden, not displayed.

I don't strip and repaint. I don't hide the dents. I clean, service, and preserve. What you get is authentic - a time capsule from mountain biking's defining years.

"I don't flip bikes. I rescue them."

It started with a rusty Zaskar I found in a barn in Austria. Everyone said strip it, repaint it, make it look new. But something felt wrong about erasing 30 years of history.

That bike had stories in its scratches. Trail dust embedded in places no brush could reach. A dent on the top tube from some forgotten crash in the 90s. Who was I to erase all that?

So I cleaned it. Serviced it. Protected the patina with a clear coat. And when I posted it online, something clicked. People didn't want showroom-perfect restorations. They wanted authenticity. They wanted time capsules.

Now I hunt for these bikes across Europe. I bring them back to life without taking away their soul. Every bike here rode trails before most of us were born. They deserve to keep their scars.

— The Curator, Pat Ina
